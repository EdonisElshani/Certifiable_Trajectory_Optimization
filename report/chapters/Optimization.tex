\subsection{Control of the Acrobot in Position-Only Maximal Coordinates}
\label{sec:control_acrobot_position_only}

The position-only formulation eliminates the translational velocity variables by
substituting
\[
v_{i,k}
=
\frac{x_{i,k+1}-x_{i,k}}{h}.
\]
Therefore, the translational dynamics are written directly in terms of neighboring
position variables \(x_{i,k-1},x_{i,k},x_{i,k+1}\). The decision variables are
\[
\left\{
x_{1,k},x_{2,k},R_{1,k},R_{2,k}
\right\}_{k=0}^{N},
\qquad
\left\{
F_{1,k},F_{2,k}
\right\}_{k=0}^{N-1},
\]
together with the Lagrange multipliers and control inputs
\[
\left\{
\lambda_{0,k},\lambda_{12,k},u_k
\right\}_{k=1}^{N-1}.
\]
In the implementation, the physical constraint force is represented by a scaled
multiplier
\[
\bar{\lambda}_{0,k}=s_\lambda \lambda_{0,k},
\qquad
\bar{\lambda}_{12,k}=s_\lambda \lambda_{12,k},
\]
where \(s_\lambda\) denotes the parameter `lambda_scale`.
